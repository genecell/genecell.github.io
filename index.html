Bio

I am a PhD student in Institute of Genetics and Developmental Biology, Chinese Academy of Sciences. Previously, I finished my Undergraduate studies in Zhejiang University.

My research interests lie in the intersection of single cell data analysis, machine learning and epigenetics. In particular, I’m now focusing on developing efficient algorithms for batch effect correction, dimensionality reduction and multimodal data integration in single cell data analysis. In addition, I’m also working on matrix decomposition, network embedding, and identifying novel genomic regulatory elements.

I am looking for postdoc opportunities and please feel free to contact me.

Recent Works

  • An Accurate and Scalable Dimensionality Reduction Method for scRNA-seq Data Analysis (in preparation)

  • An Effective Method to Correct Batch Effects among Single-cell RNA-sequencing Data using Machine Learning Approach (in preparation)

  • A Comprehensive Database of Genes Related to Repeat Expansion Diseases (in revision)
    It is a comprehensive manually-curated database that covers all reported repeat expansion diseases and also provides a list of predicted genes containing unstable tandem repeats that may cause diseases via abnormal repeat expansion by SVM and random forest.

  • A Novel Effcient Algorithm for Computing Genomic Repeats and Sequence Repeatability (in submission)

Projects

  • Novel Algorithms or Frameworks for Single-cell Data Analysis
    Recently, I spent most of time developing methods and tools for single-cell data analysis, and proposed several algorithms for dimensionality reduction, clustering, batch effect correction, and data integration. Manuscripts are under preparation. Besides, I am looking for postdoc opportunities related to single cell data analysis and machine learning.

  • Identification of a Novel Class of Regulatory Elements
    We have identified a novel class of genomic regulatory elements and are now investigating its role in mouse embryonic stem cells.

    We discovered a novel role of miRNA in translational regulation (closely related to uORF). This project is now suspended since I am busy with single cell data analysis.
